Experiment 2 - Yosemite Living Impression

Description

This experiment attempts to make a landscape reminiscent of Yosemite National Park. Last summer I went to the park with my best friend and younger brother, and I loved the beauty and tranquility of the park, especially at night. I tried to capture some of that peacefulness here.

Image inspiration

Technical

Utilizing p5.js, this experiment creates various landscapes using the built in noise function. This function creates values similar to the one before it, allowing for a more smooth surface for the mountain ranges as well as the water. The stars and trees are made using a classes that randomly place them at the the start of the program. There's also a slight bit of movement with the water, which moves with the frame count. There is also a chance to see shooting stars in the night sky if you look long enough!

Reflection

One of the tougher challenges I encountered during this experiment was with the mountain generation. I wanted to create two layers of mountains, with the one in the foreground being narrow enough so that the background mountains could be seen. I was able to find a way to have the foreground mountains slope downward about a third of the way through the page, although if you look closely you can see the slope is a bit too jagged. That being said I still like the way the mountains turned out. I also am happy with the way the night sky turned out. Originally I was planning on having the landscape take place during the day, but I decided to add some detail to the sky by making it night. This also gave me the opportunity to add the shooting stars, which I'm happy with. The water also turned out rather nicely, though I think I could've added more detail like light reflection to it. I think the least successful part of this experiment were the trees. I didn't really know the best way to add trees to the scene, so I just tried to add them in the transition from the grass areas the mountain range. But Yosemite has a lot of trees both on the mountains and the area by the water, so the lack of trees makes it less recognizable as Yosemite and more broad Sierra Nevada. I also wanted to add a waterfall to the mountain range on the left side, but I couldn't figure out how to make flowing water look good on the mountain range, especially since it generates differently depending on the seed so the waterfall would probably have to be placed somewhere differently every time.
